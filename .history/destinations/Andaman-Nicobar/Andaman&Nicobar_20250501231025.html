Welcome to Andaman & Nicobars

Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a group of islands located in the Bay of Bengal, known for their stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and rich biodiversity. The islands are a popular tourist destination, offering a range of activities such as snorkeling, scuba diving, and trekking. The capital city is Port Blair, which serves as the gateway to the islands.

Top Travel Destinations

Radhanagar Beach

Located on Havelock Island, Radhanagar Beach is famed for its pristine white sands, clear turquoise waters, and tranquil ambiance. It was ranked as Asia’s best beach by Time magazine, ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and photography.

Cellular Jail

Also known as Kala Pani, this former British colonial prison in Port Blair is now a national memorial. It offers a poignant look into India’s freedom struggle and features an emotional light and sound show in the evening.

Ross Island

Once the British administrative headquarters, Ross Island is now a historic site with ruins of old churches, offices, and homes, overgrown with roots and vines. It offers a surreal colonial-era experience and is accessible by a short ferry ride.

Elephant Beach

Located on Havelock Island, Elephant Beach is famous for its vibrant coral reefs and water sports like snorkeling, banana boat rides, and sea walking. It’s ideal for adventure lovers and marine life enthusiasts.

Baratang Island

This remote island offers unique attractions like limestone caves, dense mangrove creeks, and the rare mud volcanoes. A visit here also includes a scenic drive through the tribal reserve forest.

North Bay Island

North Bay is a top spot for water sports including snorkeling, scuba diving, and glass-bottom boat rides. The island is also featured on the Indian 20 Rupee note, showing the lighthouse in the backdrop.

Chidiya Tapu

Known as the 'Sunset Point' of Andamans, Chidiya Tapu offers lush forests, birdwatching, and stunning sunset views. It’s a peaceful getaway close to Port Blair with nature trails and a quiet beach.

Mount Harriet National Park

Mount Harriet is the third highest peak in the Andaman Islands and offers panoramic views, nature trails, and rich biodiversity. It is a haven for trekkers and home to the famous scene on the Indian 20 Rupee note.

Neil Island

Smaller and quieter than Havelock, Neil Island is known for its relaxed vibe, coral reefs, and scenic beaches like Laxmanpur, Bharatpur, and Sitapur. It's perfect for cycling, beach hopping, and stargazing.

Viper Island

Once a site for a British jail before Cellular Jail was built, Viper Island holds ruins of gallows and cells. It’s a short boat ride from Port Blair and provides a quiet historical detour.
