Welcome to Andaman & Nicobars

Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a group of islands located in the Bay of Bengal, known for their stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and rich biodiversity. The islands are a popular tourist destination, offering a range of activities such as snorkeling, scuba diving, and trekking. The capital city is Port Blair, which serves as the gateway to the islands.

Radhanagar Beach

Often hailed as one of the best beaches in Asia, Radhanagar Beach on Havelock Island is a paradise of silky white sand, turquoise water, and serene sunsets. The beach stretches along the coast, offering tranquil vibes perfect for relaxation, swimming, and photography. Visitors come here to escape the noise and lose themselves in the calm of nature.

How to Reach

First, reach Port Blair (Veer Savarkar Airport). From there, take a government or private ferry to Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep). Radhanagar Beach is about 10 km from the Havelock jetty and can be reached by cab, auto-rickshaw, or rented two-wheeler.

Cellular Jail

A historical landmark symbolizing India's freedom struggle, Cellular Jail in Port Blair, also known as Kala Pani, once housed many brave freedom fighters under British rule. Today, it stands as a solemn memorial to their sacrifices. Visitors can explore the cells, corridors, and a powerful light and sound show that brings history alive.

How to Reach

Cellular Jail is located in Port Blair, around 5 km from the Veer Savarkar Airport. You can reach it by auto-rickshaw, cab, or local buses easily within 10–15 minutes.

Ross Island

Once the administrative headquarters of the British, Ross Island is now a ghost town filled with ruins overrun by nature. The island offers a quiet walk through colonial buildings like the church, bakery, and offices. Peacocks, deer, and banyan trees add charm to this historical island surrounded by the sea.

How to Reach

Take a short 15-minute boat ride from Port Blair's Aberdeen Jetty. Boats to Ross Island operate daily, and round-trip tickets can be purchased at the jetty.

Elephant Beach

Famous for its coral reefs and vibrant marine life, Elephant Beach on Havelock Island is a hotspot for water sports. Activities like snorkeling, sea walking, and jet skiing draw tourists looking for underwater adventures amidst crystal-clear water.

How to Reach

From Havelock Island jetty, take a 20-minute boat ride or hike through the forest trail to reach Elephant Beach. Boats are available in the morning till early afternoon.

Baratang Island

A less-explored paradise, Baratang Island features natural limestone caves, thick mangrove creeks, and rare mud volcanoes. It’s a scenic retreat into the raw beauty of Andaman and home to tribal reserves and unique biodiversity.

How to Reach

Baratang is about 100 km from Port Blair. Take a government bus or private cab via the Andaman Trunk Road, passing through tribal areas under police escort.

North Bay Island

North Bay Island is popular for its coral reefs, glass-bottom boat rides, and underwater walking experiences. It is also the site of the lighthouse featured on the ₹20 currency note. A must-visit for marine life lovers.

How to Reach

Boats to North Bay Island depart from Aberdeen Jetty in Port Blair. The ride takes around 30 minutes and is often clubbed with Ross Island tours.

Chidiya Tapu

Also known as the "Sunset Point," Chidiya Tapu is famed for its magical sunsets, lush forests, and birdwatching. It’s a quiet retreat near Port Blair, where you can walk along black sand beaches and enjoy views of tropical birds and mangroves.

How to Reach

Located around 30 km from Port Blair, it takes about 45 minutes by road via cab, auto, or bike rental.

Mount Harriet National Park

The second-highest peak in the Andaman Islands, Mount Harriet offers panoramic views, lush greenery, and rare wildlife. It’s ideal for nature lovers and trekkers. The park is also featured on the old ₹20 note showing a scenic coastal view.

How to Reach

Located 20 km from Port Blair, you can reach Mount Harriet by ferry from Chatham Jetty to Bambooflat, followed by a short drive or hike.

Neil Island (Shaheed Dweep)

A serene paradise known for its relaxed vibe, Neil Island features stunning beaches like Bharatpur and Laxmanpur. It's perfect for snorkeling, cycling, and watching spectacular sunsets without the crowds.

How to Reach

Ferries run daily from Port Blair and Havelock Island to Neil Island. The ride takes about 1.5 to 2 hours depending on the vessel.

×

Viper Island

Once a site for British executions, Viper Island has eerie ruins of an old gallows and prison. The tranquil surroundings and historical relevance make it a hauntingly beautiful stop in the Andaman itinerary.

How to Reach

Take a boat from Phoenix Bay Jetty in Port Blair. It’s a short 15-20 minute ride to Viper Island.
